Rohan Chandra is currently a postdoctoral research fellow in Texas Robotics, advised by Dr. Joydeep Biswas and Dr. Peter Stone, at the University of Texas, Austin. Rohan obtained his B.Tech from the Delhi Technological University, New Delhi in 2016 and completed his MS and PhD in 2018 and 2022 from the University of Maryland advised by Dr. Dinesh Manocha. His doctoral thesis focused on autonomous driving in dense, heterogeneous, and unstructured traffic environments. Rohan is a Microsoft Future Leader in Robotics and AI, KAUST Rising Star in AI 2023, UMD’21 Future Faculty Fellow, RSS’22 Pioneer, and he is a recipient of the UMD’20 summer research fellowship. He has published his work in top computer vision and robotics conferences (CVPR, ICRA, IROS) and has participated in workshops and program committees of leading conferences in robotics, computer vision, artificial intelligence, and machine learning. He has also given invited talks at academic seminars and workshops and has served on robotics panels alongside distinguished faculty.

Research Interests

  1. Multi-agent Planning and Navigation
    2. I am broadly interested in making robots navigate safely, efficiently, and with social compliance, in challenging environments that include humans. My specific research interests within this domain span decentralized planning under private incentives, physics-informed planning, planning-constrained perception.
  2. Autonomous Driving
    3. I am also interested in continuing to extend autonomous driving to denser, heterogeneous, and more unstructured traffic environments such as that in India.
